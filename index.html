Full Guide

How to use
Hisab-Kitab.

A complete step-by-step guide so you can use every feature to its full potential — even if you're not tech-savvy.

📱 Quick tips before you start
✅ Use Chrome on Android or Safari on iPhone for the best experience
✅ Install to your home screen so it opens like a real app
✅ Sign up once — your data syncs to all your devices automatically
✅ Your data is private — only you can see it
✅ Works in areas with bad internet — offline mode saves everything
📒Recording Daily Cash (Hisab-Kitab)
1
Open the Ledger tab
Tap the 📒 icon at the bottom. This is your daily cash register. Your income, expense, and balance totals update live at the top.
2
Choose Cash In, Cash Out, or Deposit
Green "Cash In" for money received. Red "Cash Out" for money spent. Purple "Deposit" for advance payments (explained below).
3
Type the description — stock auto-fills
Start typing a stock item name and a dropdown appears showing matching items with their selling price. Tap to auto-fill both the name and price. No typing needed.
4
Pick category from quick buttons
Tap any category chip (Sales, Rent, Salary etc.) to fill it instantly. Categories change based on your business mode. Then tap Add.
The category buttons and button labels change automatically based on your Business Mode — a travel agency sees "Bookings" and "Commission", a restaurant sees "Dine-In Sales" and "Ingredients".
🕐Using the Deposit / Advance Tracker
1
What is a deposit?
When a customer pays part of the amount upfront (advance/booking fee) and the balance later — that's a deposit. Very common in Nepal for tours, salons, construction, and education.
2
Tap the "Deposit" button
In the Ledger tab, tap the purple Deposit button (third option next to Cash In / Cash Out). Extra fields appear below the form.
3
Fill in the deposit details
Enter: Customer name, service/item description, total amount of the job, and how much they're paying now (deposit). The balance due calculates automatically.
4
Set an expected payment date
Add when the remaining balance is due. This entry saves with a purple DEPOSIT badge and full details in the note field.
5
Track pending deposits
A purple summary bar appears above your entries showing total deposits received and how many are pending. When the final payment arrives, record it as a normal Cash In.
Example: A travel agent collects रू 5,000 advance for a रू 25,000 Pokhara tour. Record: Deposit = रू 5,000, Total = रू 25,000, Balance = रू 20,000 due on travel date.
🧾Creating Professional Invoices
1
Add your business logo first
Go to Settings → Business Profile → Upload Logo. Add a PNG or JPG of your shop logo. It appears on every invoice automatically.
2
Open Invoice tab → Create New
Your business name and tax ID (PAN/ABN) auto-fill from your profile. Enter the buyer's name and date.
3
Add line items with quantity and rate
The correct tax (VAT 13% for Nepal, GST 10% for Australia, etc.) calculates automatically based on your region. The total updates live as you type.
4
Save, PDF, or WhatsApp
Tap "Save & PDF" for a clean invoice PDF with your logo. Tap "WhatsApp" to send the bill text directly to the customer. Both include your Hisab-Kitab branding at the bottom.
Tap any saved invoice to mark it Paid or Unpaid. Saved invoices never disappear — your full billing history is always there.
📦Stock, Services & Business Modes
1
Set your Business Mode first
Go to Settings → Business Mode. Choose from 15 modes: Retail, Restaurant, Pharmacy, Salon, Travel, Construction, Freelance, Education, IT, Property and more. This personalises the whole app.
2
Stock-based businesses (retail, pharmacy, grocery...)
The Stock tab shows an inventory list. Add items with quantity, unit, cost price, selling price, and a low-stock alert. The tab is renamed (Stock / Inventory / Medicines / Products) to match your business.
3
Service businesses (travel, salon, freelance, IT...)
The tab switches to a Services catalogue — add your services with name, price, and description. These appear in the ledger dropdown too. Tab is renamed (Packages / Services / Projects / Courses).
4
Auto stock deduction
When you record a sale in the Ledger with the exact name of a stock item, the quantity decreases automatically. No manual update needed.
Each business mode also changes the Cash In/Out button labels, entry categories, and the tip shown in the ledger — so everything feels built for your business, not generic.
📊Reading Your Reports
1
Open the Report tab
Tap 📊. You'll see your income vs expense for the last 7 days as a bar chart.
2
Check expense categories
The bar chart below shows which category costs you the most. If Rent is 60% of expenses, that's a signal to review it.
3
View monthly tax summary
Scroll down to see each month's total sales, expenses, profit, and VAT collected. Hand this to your accountant.
4
Export or share
Go to Ledger → tap the PDF button for a printable report, or WhatsApp to share your daily summary.
The AI tab gives you instant plain-English (or Nepali) analysis of your reports. Just ask "Am I profitable this month?"
🤝Setting Up Partner / Shared Accounts
1
Owner creates the workspace
Go to Settings → Partner / Shared Account → "Create Workspace". A unique 8-character invite code is generated.
2
Share the invite code
Tap "Copy Invite Code" and send it to your business partner via WhatsApp or any message.
3
Partner joins
The partner opens the app, goes to Settings → Partner Account → "Join with Code" → enters the 8-character code.
4
Both can now see all data
Every entry, stock item, and customer is now shared. All edits show a label indicating who made the change.
Both partners can create invoices and reports using all shared entries — perfect for businesses with two owners.
🌏Setting Your Region, Tax & Business Logo
1
Set your business region
Go to Settings → Business Region. Choose your country. The app sets the right currency, tax name (VAT / GST / Sales Tax), tax rate, and tax ID label automatically.
2
What changes per region
Nepal → NPR, VAT 13%, PAN number. Australia → AUD, GST 10%, ABN. UK → GBP, VAT 20%, UTR. USA → USD, Sales Tax 8%, EIN. India → INR, GST 18%, PAN. And more.
3
Upload your business logo
Go to Settings → Business Profile → Upload Logo. Select a PNG or JPG (max 2MB, square recommended). Your logo appears on every invoice PDF and WhatsApp bill.
4
Add your tax ID and VAT/GST number
Enter your PAN/ABN and VAT/GST registration number in Settings → Tax Information. These auto-fill on every invoice so you don't type them every time.
Your region setting also affects the monthly tax summary in Reports — it shows the right tax name and calculates the correct amount collected to hand to your accountant.
📵Installing the App & Using Offline
1
Open in the right browser
Use Chrome on Android or Safari on iPhone. Other browsers may not support installation.
2
Android: Install the app
Tap the 3-dot menu (⋮) at the top right → tap "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App" → tap Install. The app icon appears on your home screen.
3
iPhone: Add to Home Screen
Tap the Share button (box with arrow) at the bottom → scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen" → tap Add in the top right.
4
Use it anywhere, even without internet
The app works fully offline. Add entries, check balances, manage stock — all with no internet. When you reconnect, everything syncs automatically.
You can also install on your laptop or desktop computer. In Chrome, look for the install icon (⊕) in the address bar.

About

Why we built
Hisab-Kitab.

Hisab-Kitab

Nepal has over 800,000 small businesses — from tea shops in Kathmandu to hardware stores in Pokhara to Nepali restaurants in Sydney and Melbourne. Most of them run entirely on paper, WhatsApp groups, and memory. There's no affordable, Nepali-language tool built for how they actually work.

We built Hisab-Kitab to change that. Our goal is simple: give every Nepali shopkeeper — wherever they are in the world — access to the same business tools that large companies use. Completely free, in their own language, and simple enough to learn in under a minute.

Hisab-Kitab is built with love for Nepal's entrepreneurial spirit. Every feature is designed specifically for how Nepali businesses operate — from 13% VAT compliance to WhatsApp bill sharing to the ability to track "Udharo" (credit) customers.
